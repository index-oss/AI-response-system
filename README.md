Road accident victims in India often do not receive medical help within the critical golden hour because accidents are reported manually and emergency services are notified late or with incorrect location details. There is currently no automated system that can instantly detect accidents, assess their severity, and alert the nearest ambulance and police for a faster, coordinated response.
This delay in detection and communication leads to preventable loss of lives, inefficient ambulance dispatch, and lack of real-time data for authorities to monitor accident-prone areas and improve response time.
